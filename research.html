Future Research Goals

Automatic Derivation of New Discoveries with Known Laws

Current data-driven approaches often identify patterns that may violate fundamental physical constraints, lacking the necessary scientific rigor for safety-critical applications. My research vision is to develop autonomous AI frameworks that utilize Large Language Models (LLMs) to identify data patterns in the form of symbolic equations derived directly from known physical laws (e.g., conservation of mass and energy). By integrating LLMs, the system can reason through established scientific principles to ensure strict physical consistency. Key applications include Process Systems Engineering for reaction kinetics and Bio-sensing for robust soft sensors.

Digital Process Lab

Physical experimentation in chemical engineering is resource-intensive and often sub-optimal due to traditional trial-and-error methods. My goal is to digitize the laboratory scenario by creating high-fidelity "Digital Twins" of experimental setups. By moving the iteration loop into a virtual environment, we can utilize AI to simulate outcomes and optimize the path to discovery using techniques like active learning and Bayesian optimization. This virtual prototyping reduces material waste and accelerates development timelines.

Image: Greenhouse/CO₂ capture

Integrating Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks (KANs) with physics-informed objectives to model greenhouse environments and CO₂-binding organic liquids. Focused on accurate, data-efficient models that respect physical laws for sustainable process optimization.

Image: Koopman dynamics

Learning Koopman observables and operators from process data to build linear surrogate models of nonlinear dynamics. These models facilitate stability analysis, controller design, and system interpretation for complex chemical processes.

  • Equation-learning networks for Koopman space.
  • Benchmarks: nonlinear reactors, process systems.
  • Integration with model predictive control frameworks.

Image: Bioreactor/mHealth

Combining Bayesian design of experiments, deep learning, and reinforcement learning to optimize bioreactors and predict health events from mHealth data. Emphasis on deployable models that respect process constraints and enable real-time decision-making.

  • Multiobjective optimization for antibody production.
  • Fault detection using autoencoders and forward-forward networks.
  • Deep RL (DDPG, PPO) for process control and health prediction.
